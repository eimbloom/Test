The Global Green Recovery from COVID-19

Countries around the world are grappling with the economic recovery from the coronavirus crisis. Several governments have included ‘green’ measures as part of economic stimulus packages, for example, in support of the development or conservation of green spaces, investing in clean energy infrastructure, providing electric vehicle incentives, or upgrading buildings to promote energy efficiency.
This page visualises how countries are approaching 'green' spending to recover from the COVID-19 crisis, in the context of their national environmental performance. Environmental performance is evaluated according to 6 metrics selected to guage a country's 1) development of a green economy, and 2) stewardship of natural resources.
The dashboard is designed to improve understanding of the context in which governments are making spending decisions, and encourage accountability in particular for countries with poorer environmental performances. The visualisations build on the data and analysis of the Green Recovery Observatory project, and cover 50 leading economies. Select an environmental performance metric from the drop-down menu to get started.

The visualisation below recreates a plot from the Global Recovery Observatory showing green recovery spending by country. The arrows are added to indicate each country's environmental performance, and to keep this in mind as part of a wider appreciation of a country's green recovery spending, adjusted for environmental performance. The length and direction of each arrow is based on a country's individual environmental performance score (a value between -1 and 1), based on the metrics shown in the plot above. A positive score gives an upward-facing arrow, and the arrow of a negative score faces downward.
